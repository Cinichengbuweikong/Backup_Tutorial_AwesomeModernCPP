# Classes and Object-Oriented Programming

Classes are C++'s mechanism for bundling "data" and "operations" together, and they form the cornerstone of object-oriented programming. In this chapter, we start with the simplest class definitions and gradually figure out how to write constructors, when destructors are called, the difference between `static` members and regular members, and what the "privileged channel" of friends is all about. Once we understand these concepts, we can write our own well-encapsulated types.
